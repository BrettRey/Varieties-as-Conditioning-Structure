## 2026-03-26

- **HTML slides for LVC talk (2026-03-27).** Created `slides.qmd` using Quarto revealjs + house style theme. Multiple versions (v1, v2, v3). Major restructuring: centred HPC theory with saber-tooth opening; replaced MTE examples with personal Japan material; replaced ballroom with quotative like / だよね; predictions-first structure.
- **Varieties aren't correlates — they're architecture.** The social variable is a parameter of the grammar itself, not an external influence. This is the ontological claim that distinguishes the framework.
- **S/A/I as cuts, not categories.** S/A/I are useful cuts through a continuous conditioning space — not exhaustive, not atomic, but not arbitrary (they track where different maintenance mechanisms operate).
- **Reciprocal relationship with LVC.** Varieties are categories that HPC theory can analyze AND the conditioning structure that makes linguistic categories projectible. The relationship goes both ways.
- **Coordination loss, not deadweight loss.** The cost of A/I mismatch is "coordination loss" (Pareto-dominated equilibrium), not "deadweight loss" (market-failure baggage, implies quantifiability). Both parties bear costs in common-interest signalling games. Ascription functions as a coarse, non-updatable correlating device (Aumann 1974).
- **Passing and raciolinguistic perception as mirror cases.** Same mechanism (A/I gap), opposite directions. The framework predicts symmetry; asymmetry reveals where power enters.
- **Dark side: pure diagnosis.** The talk diagnoses standardization as a coordination equilibrium. Prescription (what to do about it) is the audience's domain.

## 2026-03-27

- **Reframed slide 4: "The grammar is conditioned."** Avoid "varieties are architecture" (reifies what translanguaging dissolved). The claim: social context is one of the homeostatic properties in the grammar's cluster, not an overlay.
- **Avoid "appropriateness."** Flores and Rosa argue the concept is inherently normative. Use "listener expectation" or "predictive match" instead.
- **O'Connor gets her own slide before the dark side.** Coordination is a stabilizer; coordination on its own can lead to unfairness. Must be established before the dark side can use it.
- **Separated HPC contribution from S/A/I contribution.** Two distinct slides: what HPC generally adds (real without essences, boundary cases as evidence, mechanisms as explanation, reciprocity, weaken stabilizer, no conditioning gain) vs. what S/A/I specifically adds (interactional conditioning, identification separable, joint distribution, situational magnitude prediction).
- **Belfast as corroboration, not prediction.** Contrasted LVC reading (finding about this mechanism in this community) vs. HPC reading (instance of a general cross-domain prediction). Removed "exactly as predicted."
- **All divergence pairs named.** S≠A, S≠I, A≠I each produce different phenomena. S≠A is not simply "style-shifting" — it's the context for style-shifting.
- **S×A/I interaction: direction indeterminate but testable.** Stage-dependent (more shifting during transition, less after consolidation). Asymmetric at all stages (stronger suppression with target audience). Testable as random slope in mixed-effects models.
- **Divergence is stable, not degraded.** Like cattle being stable at the countability boundary — two mechanism regimes both doing their jobs. Projectibility is field-relative, not degraded.
- **"Varieties are real the way words are real."** No essences, but maintained by usage and projectible. Immediate intuition for linguists.
- **Moved TH-fronting from Belfast slide to anchor-shift slide.** TH-fronting is "add a new mechanism" not "weaken a stabilizer."
- **Brett's manual edits:** Expanded mirror-cases slide to include Bayesian framing (ascription as strong prior that blocks posterior updating). Integrated Japan restaurant directly into that slide. Rewrote divergence slide intro to connect to three waves explicitly.
- **Exogenous vs endogenous stabilizers (O'Connor integration).** S/A/I are exogenous stabilizers (from outside the interaction); coordination costs are endogenous (arise from the game itself). But they interpenetrate — ascription locks in partly because others coordinate on it. The distinction is useful, not clean.
- **Coordination costs as fourth stabilizer.** O'Connor's game theory adds a stabilizer LVC doesn't typically model. Disagree with O'Connor on primacy — coordination costs aren't *the* fundamental mechanism with S/A/I as landscape; they're interpenetrating.
- **Cultural red king effect in Belfast.** Network dissolution flipped coordination costs — minority variety speakers now encountered more outgroup members, so accommodation became the better-paying strategy. Both exogenous and endogenous stabilizers degraded simultaneously, explaining rapid levelling.
- **Variables live at the divergence points.** Features become variable precisely when S/A/I dimensions pull in different directions. The stable core is invariant because all dimensions converge. The envelope of variation should map onto the divergence structure.
- **Cut restaurant slide.** Anecdote isn't data; the Bayes point and Japan experience moved to mirror cases slide. Tighter talk.
- **Cut like/だよね slide.** Anchor shift is interesting but secondary; coordination costs story is central. Change-in-progress deferred to "next chapter."
- **Renamed "dark side" to "origins of unfairness."** Nods to O'Connor's book; says what the slide is about.
- **D&S (Drummond & Schleef 2016) contribution.** The three waves each foreground one dimension of identity at the expense of the others. S/A/I holds all three as co-present parameters whose divergence generates predictions. This resolves a tension D&S identify but can't deliver on.
