# Varieties as Conditioning Structure

**Brett Reynolds**
Humber Polytechnic & University of Toronto

## Abstract

What distinguishes register from dialect from discourse community? This paper argues that they differ not in what they are but in what participant expectation is conditioned on: SITUATION, ASCRIPTION, or IDENTIFICATION. The key move is separating ascription (what you are treated as) from identification (whose norms you orient to). Drawing on Wiese's (2023) account of communicative situations and O'Connor's (2019) game-theoretic work on how categories stabilize, I argue that varieties hang together because recurring interactional settings and social coordination keep certain bundles of forms stable. In homeostatic property cluster terms, a variety is real enough to support prediction: knowing the conditioning state lets you anticipate likely forms, and hearing the forms lets you infer the conditioning state. A worked example using Kiesling's (1998) fraternity data on the sociolinguistic variable traditionally written as (ING) shows that this framework captures structure that standard factor-group analyses conflate.

**Keywords:** sociolinguistic variation, register, dialect, discourse community, homeostatic property clusters, coordination games, Bayesian conditioning, participant expectation, indexicality, ascription, identification
